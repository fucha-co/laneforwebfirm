WHY WAYSIDE?



Yeah they’re unreal. They help me with clothes. I shower there sometimes. The Cross has changed a lot. I keep to myself mostly. I’ve slept 12 straight Winters on the streets but I’m getting too old for this you know. Wayside get me referrals. I used to wear 32 jeans but now I wear 34’s. The Cross is the only place I know where you can gain weight sleeping rough. Cheap coffee too. I know Graham. I like him. Sometimes I just have to. They’re nice people in there. I haven’t been home for 3 days cause I can’t get there without money. I lent a friend some money so now I have to stay here til I find him or some more. I don’t know how I’ll get home.


Why Wayside? What a good question Laura; and in just two words too! It got me to thinking: so I went to the Cross on the weekend and asked it of some people who can answer it with a perspective I don’t know. Their position in life is a perspective that most of us never know… because most of us just never ask. But it’s so easy to ask and asking changes everything. A simple question with just two words can turn a never-known stranger-of-the-street into someone we know and someone with a story. And someone with a story is someone we can connect to because we’re all human and we all have stories. Stories are powerful — the good ones reach in to touch our hearts and shape our minds. Every story starts by invitation and communication.


While the answers above are not mine they are very real. And well-filtered. The reality of life is always as confronting and uncomfortable as it is beautiful and wonderful and the spectrum of answers I heard to the question “Why Wayside?” were no different. I don’t pretend to know Wayside well — I know it a long-established, well-respected organisation providing support to those who truly need it. And I trust in all it takes to build and maintain such name in this modern world. I’ve invested some extra time reading and watching what I can of Wayside as well as speaking directly to some who use your service. But this is not the first time I’ve stopped and asked questions of strangers-of-the-street, nor helped beyond that. I’ve done neither enough in my life but I have had, as Graham would say, a “lived life”; one that’s never been motivated by money and one that’s forever been motived by much more.


I believe Wayside and I are a great culture fit with well aligned values and that I can learn as much from you as I can offer you. Lane for Wayside was “A Personal Application for a Personal Position” from the second I saw your ad on SEEK, because I was wasn’t looking for job… I was looking for much more.
