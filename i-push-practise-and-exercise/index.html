I PUSH, PRACTISE, AND EXERCISE



Away from work I love to run — running is my thing. Before I ran I rode, competitively, winning some great races and beating some great riders. I find physical challenge as rewarding as the creative kind and pushing my mind and body ultimately brings out the best in both. I once rode 3,300km (solo!) in 21 days for a personal challenge (raising money for charity along the way). Currently I practice mindfulness, run regularly, drink rarely, drug never, fast intermittently, and Wim Hof daily (per video below).



tl;dw — Wimm Hof is redefining the science of human physiology through the power of his mind. It’s almost unbelievable (and I don’t use adverbs lightly! Or do I?). Part of Wim’s daily routine is cold water therapy. It’s also part of mine.


When I’m not running, writing, or shivering Iceman-style I can be found strumming a ukulele, solving a Rubik’s Cube, or streaming a podcast or two. I love to learn. Learning sign language (Auslan Level I and II) has been a highlight in recent times and it’s a skill I’m desperate to keep practising and improving. Sign language, like other languages and communication forms, connects people, and I love that too.
